OrbiSave Financial Infrastructure & 
Payment Integration 
Focus: Financial Infrastructure, Payment Integration, and Security 
Audience: Product Team, Finance Team, System Architects, Regulatory Compliance Team 
1. Overview of Financial Infrastructure 
OrbiSave operates as a hybrid financial platform. While it orchestrates all contributions, 
payouts, and loan disbursements, actual funds are held in trust accounts with partner banks 
in each 
country. This separation ensures regulatory compliance and protects user funds while 
allowing 
automated digital management of transactions. 
The financial infrastructure layer ensures: - Secure custody of funds - Transparency and auditability - Automation of recurring contributions and payouts - Integration with mobile money and banking systems 
2. Trust Accounts 
Each country will have a dedicated OrbiSave Trust Account held by a licensed bank. These 
accounts 
will serve as the primary custody layer for user contributions and group wallets. - Kenya Trust Account – holds KES - Rwanda Trust Account – holds RWF - Ghana Trust Account – holds GHS 
Functions of the trust accounts: - Receive all member contributions - Execute payouts to members based on rotation schedules - Disburse loans approved by group governance and platform verification - Facilitate reconciliations and reporting to regulators 
Partner banks will be selected based on stability, reliability, mobile integration support, and 
compliance with local financial regulations. 
3. Payment Integration 
To automate contributions and disbursements, OrbiSave will integrate directly with the 
leading 
mobile money providers in each country. 
Kenya: - M-Pesa via Safaricom Daraja API - Airtel Money via Airtel API - Optional: Bank Paybill integration with KCB, Equity Bank 
Rwanda: - MTN MoMo via MTN API - Airtel Money (subject to feasibility) 
Ghana: - MTN MoMo via MTN API - Additional integrations to be evaluated based on local research 
The integration layer will handle: - Initiating STK Push requests for scheduled contributions - Confirming payment completion via API callbacks - Recording successful payments in the digital ledger - Reconciling payment records with the bank trust accounts - Triggering automatic payouts and loan disbursements 
This layer ensures seamless, automated transactions with minimal manual intervention. 
4. Contribution and Payout Workflow 
1. Member contribution is scheduled via the platform. 
2. Platform triggers a mobile money STK Push or bank API transaction. 
3. Payment is deposited into the country-specific trust account. 
4. The contribution is recorded in the group wallet ledger. 
5. Rotation savings pool and loan pool allocations are automatically computed. 
6. When it is a member's turn, the payout is automatically calculated, including OrbiSave 
service 
fees, and disbursed through mobile money or bank transfer. 
Service fees are charged only on completed payouts, separate from mobile money charges. 
For example, in Kenya, KES 0.03 per shilling contributed is deducted from the payout. 
5. Loan Disbursement and Repayment Flow 
The loan engine manages all internal group lending processes. Loans are funded solely from 
group loan pools and are not injected with external capital. 
Loan request workflow: 
1. Member submits a loan request via the platform. 
2. Platform verifies contribution history and loan pool availability. 
3. Chairperson and Treasurer approve the request based on group governance rules. 
4. Platform administrators perform a second verification for compliance and fraud 
prevention. 
5. Approved loans are disbursed via the trust account through mobile money or bank 
transfer. 
6. Repayments are tracked in the ledger and credited back to the group loan pool. 
Optional: Once sufficient financial history is established, groups may access external bank 
loans 
using aggregated contribution data as proof of financial reliability. 
6. Digital Ledger and Reconciliation 
The OrbiSave digital ledger records every financial transaction: - Contributions - Payouts - Loan disbursements - Loan repayments - Service fees - Governance approvals 
Each ledger entry includes timestamp, member ID, group ID, transaction type, and 
associated 
metadata. Automated reconciliation compares ledger entries with bank trust account 
statements 
and mobile money confirmations to detect discrepancies. 
This system ensures accuracy, transparency, and audit readiness for both internal and 
regulatory 
reporting. 
7. Security Architecture 
Security is a core requirement for OrbiSave due to the sensitive financial data handled. 
1. Authentication and Identity Verification: - Password and optional biometric login - Two-factor authentication (2FA) - KYC verification via secure API providers (e.g., Smile Identity, Prembly) - Mobile SIM registration verification for additional identity assurance 
2. Transaction Security: - Transaction PIN for each financial action - Dual authorization for loan disbursements (Chairperson + Treasurer + Platform admin) - Audit logs for all financial transactions - Fraud detection algorithms for anomalous transactions 
3. Data Protection: - Encryption at rest and in transit - Role-based access control - Regular penetration testing and security reviews 
8. Multi-Country and Regulatory Compliance 
Each country operates independently to comply with local regulations: - Group activities are country-restricted to prevent cross-border contribution issues. - Trust accounts comply with banking regulations in each country. - Platform only provides software infrastructure and governance; it does not operate as a 
bank or 
microfinance institution. - Automated service fees are documented in platform T&Cs for legal transparency. - Regulatory compliance includes reporting to authorities where necessary and maintaining 
full audit 
trails of contributions, payouts, and loans. 
9. Offline and Low Connectivity Support 
In low-network regions, the platform allows offline recording of group activities, including: - Attendance at meetings - Governance decisions and votes - Loan approvals 
Once connectivity is restored, offline records synchronize automatically with the central 
ledger. 
Financial transactions (contributions, payouts, loans) require network access but are 
queued for 
automatic execution once connectivity is available. 
10. Scaling and Future Expansion 
The platform is designed to support: - Millions of users and hundreds of thousands of groups - High transaction volumes - Integration with additional mobile money providers and banks - Advanced analytics for contributions, loan performance, and member behavior 
Future features may include: - Group-based credit scoring - Insurance or savings investment options for groups - Integration with formal banking and microfinance products - Enhanced digital meeting features (recordings, minutes, voting analytics)
