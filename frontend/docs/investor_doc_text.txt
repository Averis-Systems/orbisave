Seasonal Cash Flow Mismatch: Farmers earn in 2-3 harvest windows annually but need working capital year-round. Existing financial products demand fixed monthly contributions—structurally incompatible with agricultural income patterns.2. No Safe Savings Infrastructure: Banks require documentation, collateral, minimum balances, and physical branches. 75% of rural Kenyans are excluded from formal banking. Mobile wallets (M-Pesa) offer convenience but zero savings discipline.3. Paper-Based Group Vulnerability: Traditional chamas rely on manual cash collection, paper ledgers prone to errors/disputes/fraud, single treasurers holding all cash, and no verifiable financial history for future credit access.4. Financial Shock Destruction: Without savings buffers, farmers remain one illness, drought, or equipment failure away from destitution—permanently trapped in poverty cycles.1.2 Why Every Existing Solution Falls ShortSolution TypePromiseCritical Failure for FarmersCommercial BanksSafe deposits, creditRequire ID, collateral, urban branches;
