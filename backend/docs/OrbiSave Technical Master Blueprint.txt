OrbiSave Technical Master Blueprint  
Platform OrbiSave 
Purpose Technical and Architectural Documentation for Team Alignment 
1. Introduction 
OrbiSave is a digital financial infrastructure platform designed to support community 
savings groups 
(commonly known as chamas, VSLAs, farmer cooperatives, corporate saving groups, and 
informal 
community finance circles). The platform provides a secure technological environment 
where groups 
can manage rotating savings contributions, internal loan pools, automated payouts, and 
financial 
records using mobile money integrations and bank custody systems. 
Unlike traditional fintech platforms that directly hold or manage customer deposits, 
OrbiSave operates 
as a financial orchestration and governance infrastructure. Funds contributed by users are 
stored within 
trust accounts held by partner banks in each operating country. The platform itself 
maintains digital 
ledgers, governance mechanisms, and payment orchestration that enable secure 
transactions without 
assuming direct custodianship of the funds. 
The first markets targeted for deployment are Kenya, Rwanda, and Ghana. Each market will 
operate 
independently with localized payment integrations, trust accounts, regulatory alignment, 
and currency 
management. 
2. Platform Vision and Strategic Role 
OrbiSave aims to digitize and modernize the traditional savings group model which has 
existed across 
Africa for decades. Millions of individuals rely on rotating savings groups to pool funds, 
provide short‑ 
term loans, and create financial safety nets in the absence of formal banking access. 
The goal of OrbiSave is not to replace this culture but to enhance it through automation, 
transparency, 
and secure digital infrastructure. 
The platform focuses on five strategic outcomes 
1. Eliminating manual bookkeeping and calculation errors. 
2. Preventing financial mismanagement through automated governance. 
3. Enabling automated contributions and payouts via mobile money. 
4. Creating verifiable financial histories for group members. 
5. Enabling future integration with formal banking and lending institutions. 
Through this approach, OrbiSave becomes a bridge between informal community finance 
and formal 
financial institutions. 
3. Hybrid Financial Infrastructure Model 
OrbiSave operates using a hybrid financial infrastructure model. This means that the 
platform does not 
store user funds directly but instead orchestrates transactions while banks hold the funds 
in trust 
accounts. 
In this architecture, every contribution made by a group member through mobile money or 
bank 
transfer is routed into a trust account that belongs to OrbiSave but is legally safeguarded 
under bank 
custody rules. 
The platform then records the contribution within its internal ledger system and allocates 
the funds to 
the corresponding group wallet. 
This model ensures that 
• Customer funds remain protected under regulated banking institutions. 
• The platform avoids being classified as a deposit‑taking financial institution. 
• The system maintains full traceability and financial auditability. 
• Transaction automation remains possible without compromising regulatory safety. 
The hybrid model is widely used in financial infrastructure platforms where software 
providers manage 
the operational layer while licensed financial institutions manage the custody layer. 
4. Trust Account Architecture 
In each country of operation, OrbiSave will establish dedicated trust accounts within 
partner banks. 
These accounts act as the primary custody layer for all funds contributed by users within 
that country. 
For example 
Kenya Trust Account – holds all Kenyan contributions in Kenyan Shillings. 
Rwanda Trust Account – holds all Rwandan contributions in Rwandan Francs. 
Ghana Trust Account – holds all Ghanaian contributions in Ghanaian Cedis. 
When a group member contributes funds, the payment is routed into the respective trust 
account. 
OrbiSave then records the transaction in the digital ledger and assigns the value to the 
appropriate 
group wallet. 
Trust accounts will also execute outgoing transactions including 
• Loan disbursements 
• Member payout rotations 
• Refunds or dispute resolutions 
Banks therefore act as the financial custodian while OrbiSave operates the digital 
infrastructure and 
governance systems that instruct the transactions. 
5. Payment Infrastructure and Mobile Money Integration 
Mobile money is the dominant financial transaction method across the target countries. 
OrbiSave will 
integrate directly with the major mobile money systems in each market to automate 
contributions and 
payouts. 
In Kenya, integration will include M‑Pesa and Airtel Money. Contributions will primarily be 
initiated 
through automated STK Push requests triggered by the platform on scheduled contribution 
dates. 
In Rwanda and Ghana, MTN Mobile Money will be the primary integration partner for early 
deployment. 
The payment orchestration layer within OrbiSave performs the following tasks 
• Initiating contribution payment requests. 
• Verifying transaction completion through provider APIs. 
• Recording payment confirmations within the internal ledger. 
• Reconciling payments with the bank trust accounts. 
• Triggering payouts or loan disbursements when approved. 
This payment orchestration layer is a critical system component because it connects the 
digital 
platform with real financial rails. 
6. Digital Ledger System 
At the core of the platform lies the OrbiSave Digital Ledger System. 
The ledger is responsible for recording every financial event within the platform including 
• Member contributions 
• Allocation to payout pools 
• Allocation to loan pools 
• Loan disbursements 
• Loan repayments 
• Service fee deductions 
• Payout transactions 
Each entry in the ledger creates a permanent financial record linked to both the member 
and the 
group wallet. 
The ledger ensures that all balances can be traced, verified, and audited at any time. This 
structure is 
essential for maintaining transparency and trust among group members as well as external 
financial 
partners.
